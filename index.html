Background & Goals

Re:Form explores reducing human priors in scalable formal software verification by leveraging reinforcement learning (RL) in large language models (LLMs). This study uses Dafny as a case to demonstrate how automated reasoning can be enhanced with minimal human intervention.

Method & Technology

Our approach integrates RL techniques with LLMs to automate the process of formal verification. Dafny, a language for program verification, serves as the testbed. The system iteratively refines verification strategies, learning from feedback and reducing reliance on handcrafted human priors.

Results & Outlook

Preliminary results show promising improvements in verification scalability and reduced manual effort. Future work will focus on expanding to other verification tools and further optimizing RL strategies within LLMs.
